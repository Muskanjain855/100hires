## Post: Turning the company into a video game using Fable 5

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ericosiu_i-used-fable-5-to-turn-my-company-into-a-activity-7478794763232772096-MtHx

Full text: COLLECTED 

I used Fable 5 to turn my company into a video game.

The result is a Business Command Center.

It has a main dashboard, a daily empire briefing, connectors, quests, and different modes for B2B SaaS vs B2B services.

The company becomes a map.

If product signups are down, the Product Factory is on fire.

If MRR is up, coins start coming out of the Finance Base.

If marketing data is missing, that part of the map is covered in fog.

The more connectors you add, the smarter the map gets. Stripe, HubSpot, Gong, Mixpanel, GA4, Ahrefs, Metricool. Each one gives the system more context.

Then it turns business problems into quests.

Repair the activation funnel. Revive stalled deals. Fix failed payments. Train a new teammate in the Skills Dojo.

This is how I’ve always seen business anyway.

You are constantly finding bottlenecks, fixing them, making sure they stay fixed, and moving to the next constraint.

Dashboards show you what happened.

A Business Command Center shows your team what quest to take next.

That is where I think business software is going.

Work becomes more visible. Ownership becomes clearer. Progress becomes playable.

And eventually, companies may be able to open parts of their world to outsiders, letting people take on quests, build skills, earn reputation, and trade with the business directly.

I’m going to keep building this.

If you’re running a company, would you rather have another dashboard or a living map of what needs to be fixed next?

## Post: Turning AI adoption into a leaderboard

Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ericosiu_we-turned-ai-adoption-into-a-leaderboard-activity-7478141633105076224-3--l

Full text: COLLECTED 

We turned AI adoption into a leaderboard and the whole team has bought in.

AI adoption is a difficult thing to solve.

How do you get company buy-in?
How do you combat AI theater?
How do you set the standard for what good actually looks like?

For the last few months AI adoption has been top of mind for us. One way we tackled this was building an internal skills marketplace where anyone can publish the automations they make and install the ones their teammates build.

Everyone liked the idea of it. But in practice most people kept doing their work by hand and the good automations just lived on whoever built them.

Then we added rankings. The most-used skills climbed to the top, and your name sat right next to whatever you shipped. Just like Reddit.

Nothing else changed. The only new thing was a scoreboard, and suddenly everyone wanted to be on it.

Automating your own job can feel a little risky, like you're making your own role smaller. But "gamifying" it by adding a marketplace and a leaderboard makes it like playing a video game.

And you get people sitting on their best automations to racing to put them up.

Turns out the fastest way to get people to adopt AI is to make it fun :)
